PORTFOLIO
Most recently I've worked for Bose, a audio company that focuses on high quality sound. The issue with being well-known for high quality products was that there would be a number of counterfeit products in the market. In order to determine the identity of their devices, Bose created a Cloud development team. From this starting point, various other services such as IoT, OTA updates, and Voice Assistants became a part of the products being sold.
At Bose, I worked on the DevOps team supporting developers in their transitions from development to production, maintaining those services in production, and providing KPIs for higher ups. Some of my key projects are noted in my Resume.
Carnegie Mellon is once of the few universities that have an undergraduate Chemical Engineering lab. This allows the undergrad ChemE students to study various examples of Mass Transfer and Unit Operation during their undergraduate career. After taking one of these classes, I spent a summer working as a lab tech for the Rothfus lab, where I worked hands-on with decades old units, repairing and cleaning them for use in later labs. Mid-summer, I began work at IBM but I still took time to work on developing a new inventory maintenance system using an opensource store-front webapp.
I worked for the Hybrid Cloud division of IBM, where I worked on developing a production deployment pipeline for cloud services. Since there was no standard for repository structure, every service was structured differently. This meant that we needed to create a library to find the correct locations of each file necessary for deployment. This library would be used in Jenkins, but needed to be able to be ported to a different CI management tools, so portable bash scripts were used and a groovy library was created to implement them. This project ended up turning a process that could take days into one that only took a few minutes.
I took time my sophomore year to work for a Professor to help them with their research project. I worked to expand the Pyomo library by implementing McCormick envelopes. A McCormick envelope is a transformation of bilinear optimization problems used to relax them into convex problems, which are much easier to solve and require less computational resources to reach comparable results.
Out of high school and in my first summer as a college student, I worked at Perrigo Nutritionals to provide QA testing for powdered baby formula. A number of assays were performed on raw, in-process, and finished materials to ensure that they conformed to various FDA set regulations. A number of experiments were performed, but my main focus was placed on HPLC analysis of Vitamin A, LECO protein analysis, and lipid analysis with ethyl and petroleum ethers.
My freshman year I worked on Rock Climber, a video game. This was a term project in which I created a video game using python's tkinter library for animation. The game also implemented several storage and display optimizations to ensure that it ran efficiently throughout the entire play period.
If I had time, I would work as a camp counselor for the Governor's Institute of Vermont, a camp oriented towards teaching kids about various applications of mathematics. To this end, I organized lectures to teach campers about game theory and probability. I also developed software to supplement a lecture. Led high-school campers through a college campus. Finally, I advised students in college decisions.
In my time as a camper, I worked with a partner to create a remote controlled vehicle and developed code to connect input from a playstation controller to movements of the vehicle. The vehicle was then presented at the city mall with several other creations.
I also volunteered time for the Winooski Conservation District, helping them gather data on the watershed by collecting weekly samples. The samples were used to test for Sodium Chloride levels and determine the effects of road salting on the health of the surrounding environment.
